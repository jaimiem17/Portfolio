2. Data Abstraction from "The Fallen of WWII"

I was a little bit unsure of the dataset but at first I'd say that it is an example of a Set because the idea was to just use the item, a person's figure, to represent the amount of casualities of the war without any order or specifity of placement. Later in the video the data started resembling more of a barchart/table like dataset because there were attributes under each set of data and the height/amount of items were still representing the casualities but they were categorized. Each item in the dataset represents a death in WWII. A categorical attribute were the countries that were shown in the video. The quantitative-ratio attribute was the amount of casualities. An ordinal attribute was the years shown on the timeline of the war. Another attribute was the difference in casualties of US soldiers that died from fighting the Nazis vs fighting the Japanese. This is quantitative-ratio. The people that the US fought specifically, the Japanese and Nazis, are also categorical attributes. From the data shown, I can derive that the amount a Nazi deaths were the Germany deaths plus the data from Hungary, Romania, and Italy because those countaries were labeled as Nazi axis powers as well.
